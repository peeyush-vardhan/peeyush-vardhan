# Leadership Principles

These principles are written to reflect a strategic product leadership style without overstating achievements. Adapt the language so it mirrors how you actually operate.

## 1. Start With the Problem

I begin with the user problem, the business constraint, and the market context before discussing solutions. Strong product decisions come from clear problem framing.

## 2. Build Shared Context

Cross-functional execution improves when teams understand the why behind the roadmap. I value product communication that reduces ambiguity and creates alignment early.

## 3. Prioritize for Impact

Not every opportunity deserves investment. I favor decisions that connect effort to strategic outcomes, customer value, and learning velocity.

## 4. Treat Data as Decision Support

Metrics matter, but they are most useful when paired with judgment, customer insight, and operational context. I use data to sharpen decisions, not to outsource them.

## 5. Make Execution Legible

Teams move faster when decisions, tradeoffs, and owners are visible. I believe in clear product artifacts, explicit priorities, and crisp communication.

## 6. Collaborate Across Functions

Product leadership is not isolated ownership. The strongest outcomes come from thoughtful collaboration with engineering, design, analytics, GTM, and leadership stakeholders.

## 7. Learn Through Delivery

Shipping creates information. I value iterative learning, measured experiments, and retrospectives that improve both product quality and team judgment.
